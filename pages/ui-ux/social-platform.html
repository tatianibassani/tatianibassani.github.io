Social Media Platform

Building meaningful connections in digital spaces

Role: UX Design Lead
Duration: 9 months
Team: 5 designers, 8 developers, 3 PMs

The Challenge

Create a social platform that prioritizes meaningful interactions over engagement metrics. Users were experiencing social media fatigue from algorithm-driven feeds and wanted more authentic connections with friends and communities that shared their interests.

User Research

We conducted extensive research to understand social media behavior:

  • In-depth interviews with 45 users across different age groups
  • Social media usage diary studies over 2 weeks
  • Focus groups on privacy concerns and digital wellbeing
  • Analysis of competitor platforms and user migration patterns

Key insight: Users wanted control over their experience and genuine connections rather than viral content.

Design Principles

User Control

Chronological feeds, granular privacy settings, and algorithm transparency options.

Authentic Sharing

Tools that encourage genuine expression over performative content creation.

Digital Wellbeing

Built-in usage tracking and mindful interaction features to promote healthy habits.

Key Innovations

Interest Circles

Private groups based on shared interests with focused discussion threads.

Mindful Moments

Pause prompts and reflection tools to encourage thoughtful engagement.

Privacy by Design

Default privacy settings and clear data usage transparency throughout the experience.

Design Examples
